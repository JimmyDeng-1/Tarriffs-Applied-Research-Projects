# Tariffs: Is the World Treating the USA Unfairly?

## Project Overview
This project analyzes global trade tariff rates to determine whether the United States faces unfair trade imbalances, as claimed by various economic perspectives. The research focuses on identifying which countries and products contribute most to these imbalances and evaluates what "fairness" looks like in a global trade context.

## Key Objectives
* **Identify Imbalances:** Analyze the difference between tariffs the US imposes vs. tariffs imposed on US exports.
* **Product Breakdown:** Pinpoint specific product categories most affected by high tariff rates.
* **Country Analysis:** Focus on significant trade partners (e.g., China, Canada, Mexico) to see where the largest disparities exist.
* **Data Visualization:** Use R Studio to create plots that illustrate patterns in trade policy.

## Tools & Data
* **Language:** R (for data cleaning and visualization)
* **Dataset:** 2022 Tariff Data from the World Integrated Trade Solution (WITS).
* **Documentation:** Policy analysis and reporting completed in Microsoft Word.

## Conclusion
The analysis aims to provide a data-backed answer to whether the US is "tariffed" more heavily than it tariffs others, moving beyond rhetoric to look at the actual trade figures.
